<p><p>Access control systems are classified into two main market types: card-based and biometrics. Card-based systems rely on physical cards with embedded chips or magnetic stripes to grant access to users. These cards can be easily distributed and replaced, making them a cost-effective option for many organizations. Biometric systems, on the other hand, rely on unique physical characteristics such as fingerprints or iris scans to verify a user's identity. While biometrics offer a higher level of security, they can be more expensive to implement and maintain.</p></p>
