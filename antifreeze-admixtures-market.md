<p><p>Antifreeze admixtures are chemicals added to concrete to lower its freezing point and prevent frost damage. There are two main types of antifreeze admixtures: powder and liquid. Powder antifreeze admixtures are typically used in cold weather conditions and are added directly to the dry mix before mixing with water. Liquid antifreeze admixtures are added to the concrete mix during mixing and are stored in liquid form. Both types help improve the durability and longevity of concrete in cold temperatures.</p></p>
