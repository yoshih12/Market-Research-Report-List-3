<p><p>Film Screen Mammography involves the use of X-ray film to capture images of the breast tissues, which are then examined by a radiologist. This type of screening equipment has been widely used for many years and is considered a reliable method for detecting breast cancer. In contrast, Digital Mammography uses electronic sensors to capture and store images of the breast tissues, providing better image quality and the ability to manipulate the images for more accurate diagnoses. Digital mammography is gradually replacing film screen mammography in the market due to its improved efficiency and image quality.</p></p>
