<p><p>Compound fertilisers are classified into two main types based on the number of essential plant nutrients they contain. Two-element compound fertilisers include combinations of two essential nutrients such as nitrogen and phosphorus or phosphorus and potassium. Three-element compound fertilisers contain a mix of three essential nutrients like nitrogen, phosphorus, and potassium. These different types of compound fertilisers cater to the varying nutrient requirements of different crops and help improve soil fertility and crop yields efficiently.</p></p>
