<p><p>Creosote is commonly divided into two main types: wood-tar creosote and coal-tar creosote. Wood-tar creosote is derived from the distillation of wood, particularly pine tar, and is often used for preserving wood and as a traditional medicine. Coal-tar creosote, on the other hand, is a byproduct of coal processing and is used primarily as a wood preservative. Both types of creosote have similar properties and applications, but they vary in production methods and sources.</p></p>
