<p><p>Food preservatives are categorized into two main types: antimicrobials and antioxidants. Antimicrobials work by inhibiting the growth of microorganisms such as bacteria, mold, and yeast in food products. This helps extend shelf life and maintain food safety. Antioxidants, on the other hand, prevent oxidation and the spoilage of food by neutralizing free radicals that cause food to go bad. Both types of preservatives play a crucial role in the food industry by extending the shelf life of products and maintaining their quality.</p></p>
