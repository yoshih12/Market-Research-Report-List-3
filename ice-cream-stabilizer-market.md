<p><p>Ice-cream stabilizers are additives that help improve the texture, consistency, and shelf life of ice cream. The main types of stabilizers used in the market include gelatin, guar gum, locust bean gum, xanthan gum, pectin, and other various stabilizers. Gelatin is derived from animal collagen and provides a smooth and creamy texture. Guar gum and locust bean gum are plant-based stabilizers that help prevent ice crystals from forming. Xanthan gum and pectin are also commonly used to improve texture and stability. Other stabilizers may include carrageenan, agar, or cellulose gum.</p></p>
