<p><p>Macrolide drugs are classified into three main types based on the number of atoms in their lactone ring structure: 14-membered ring agents, 15-membered ring agents, and 16-membered ring agents. Each type has distinct properties and uses in medical treatment. Ketolides, a subgroup of macrolide antibiotics, are characterized by a ketone substitution at the C-3 position of the macrolide ring. They are known for their enhanced activity against certain strains of bacteria, making them an important option in treating bacterial infections.</p></p>
