<p><p>Presbyopia correction devices are categorized into three main types: corneal inlays, scleral implants, and others. Corneal inlays are small lenses implanted in the cornea to improve near vision. Scleral implants are tiny devices inserted into the sclera to reshape the cornea and correct presbyopia. Other devices may include contact lenses, eyeglasses, or surgical procedures like monovision. Each type of device offers a unique approach to addressing presbyopia and improving near vision for individuals experiencing age-related vision changes.</p></p>
