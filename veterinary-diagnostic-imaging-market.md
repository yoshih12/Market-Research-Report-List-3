<p><p>Veterinary Diagnostic Imaging refers to different methods used to visualize internal structures of animals. X-ray imaging uses electromagnetic radiation to produce images of bones and tissues. Ultrasound uses sound waves to create images of soft tissues and organs. MRI, which stands for Magnetic Resonance Imaging, uses powerful magnets and radio waves to generate detailed pictures of internal organs. Other types of imaging include CT scans and endoscopy. Each method has its own advantages and is used based on the specific needs of the animal patient.</p></p>
